The Issues (v2.0)

We do not hold "views" based on moral sentiment. We hold "positions" based on system stability and throughput. If a policy reduces friction and latency, we commit it. If it increases technical debt, we revert it.

1. Gun Control

The Glitch: Deadlock between unenforceable bans (a firewall that leaks) and unregulated chaos (buffer overflow).
Roh (The Conscience) Current violence disproportionately harms the vulnerable. The wealthy can buy private security; the poor cannot.
LKY (The Architect) Disorder is unacceptable. A society where citizens must arm themselves is a failed state. The monopoly on violence belongs to the state.
Franklin (The Hacker) "A well-regulated militia." The key is *regulated*. Liberty requires responsibility. If you own a dangerous tool, you insure it.

The Patch: Liability & Licensing

1. Mandatory Liability Insurance: Treat firearms like vehicles. Every gun owner must carry liability insurance. The market will price high-risk hardware out of casual ownership.

2. Universal Registry (Blockchain): Every firearm is a registered node. Transfer without update is a felony.

2. Climate Change

The Glitch: Relying on moral shaming to stop physics. We are debating "degrowth" while the server room overheats.
Roh (The Conscience) The poor suffer first from climate collapse. Justice demands action before the system crashes.
LKY (The Architect) Survival is non-negotiable. If the water rises, the nation drowns. We do not have the luxury of debating the "perfect" solution.
Franklin (The Hacker) "An ounce of prevention is worth a pound of cure." But when prevention fails, invention must succeed. Hack the planet.

The Patch: The Energy Hard Fork

1. Nuclear Baseload: Re-open every nuclear plant. Build SMRs (Small Modular Reactors). It is the only zero-carbon source that scales.

2. Carbon Pricing Algorithm: Hard-code the externality. If you emit, you pay. The market optimizes the rest.

3. Immigration

The Glitch: A firewall that blocks high-value packets (talent) while leaking indiscriminately at the edges.
Roh (The Conscience) Fair process is mandatory. We cannot brutalize migrants, but rules must apply to all equally.
LKY (The Architect) Talent is the world's most scarce resource. Brain drain the planet. But we cannot have a welfare state with open borders.
Franklin (The Hacker) "Where liberty dwells, there is my country." The cosmopolite seeks the best minds to build the republic.

The Patch: The Talent Protocol

1. Points-Based Kernel: If you add value to the GDP (coding, engineering, medicine), you get a visa instantly.

2. Digital Nomad Visas: Frictionless entry for remote workers. Strong border enforcement combined with open doors for talent.

4. Economy & Automation

The Glitch: Human labor is being deprecated by AI. The old "work for survival" loop is breaking.
Roh (The Conscience) It is foul play to let capital capture 100% of the automation dividend while workers starve.
LKY (The Architect) Do not subsidize inefficiency. If a job can be automated, automate it. Retrain the human for higher-order tasks.
Franklin (The Hacker) When industry is automated, the citizen must become an investor in the commonwealth.

The Patch: The Citizen Stack

1. Universal Basic Compute (UBC): Tax the automation compute (GPU cycles) and distribute a "Citizen's Dividend."

2. Abolish Minimum Wage (Conditional): If the Dividend covers survival, remove the price floor on labor to maximize flexibility.

5. Abortion & Rights

The Glitch: The state trying to legislate the biological kernel.

The Patch: Biological Sovereignty

1. Codify Privacy: The state has no root access to the individual biological instance.

2. Support: Massive subsidies for contraception and natal care. Reduce the need for the procedure through system support.

6. Voting & Governance

The Glitch: Analog trust in a digital age. Doubt and conspiracy theories are crashing the system.

The Patch: Cryptographic Verification

1. National ID (Keypair): Every citizen receives a cryptographic keypair. Free and automatic.

2. Open Source Voting: All voting machines must run open-source code, auditable by any citizen. Paper trails mandatory.
